Playa del Carmen

About Playa del Carmen

Playa del Carmen is a city in the state of Quintana Roo, Mexico. It is home to over 300,000 people and is growing quite fast with an annual rate at 7.5%. The city is a popular tourist spot due to the beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and the rainforest surrounding the area.

My Experience

It's been just over a year since I visited Playa del Carmen back in August 2024. At the start of 2024, I had no idea that I would be going on this trip. It really turned out to be a last minute trip that I agreed to go on with some family. That being said, I was really lucky that the family members I went with had been planning for some time, and had an itinerary full of activities to do. I was also lucky that some of my family members had done this before, so they were already familiar with how to get around. I had never gone on a full trip to Mexico before, so this was definitely comforting. They wanted to go to Playa del Carmen instead of the nearby Cancun because of the city's proximity to a bunch of cool outdoor activities that I'll talk about more later.

My Favorite Parts of the Trip

XCaret

As I mentioned before, I decided to go on this trip last minute. Because of this, I pretty much had zero knowledge about any of the activities we were doing, including this one. XCaret is a popular theme/water park in Mexico that provides a wide variety of attractions. While there, we swam through underground rivers, walked through a nice butterfly garden, ate at a buffet on the beach, and ended the day by watching a performance in the park's theater. It was honestly an amazing experience that I would love to do again if given the chance, especially since we really couldn't get to everything due to time.

Xenotes

This excursion is one that wasn't actually planned by anybody intitially. After visiting a couple cenotes, my little cousin really wanted to see more. We had been seeing a bunch of tour busses take off that said "Xenotes," so we looked into it and decided to give it a try. We definitely were not disappointed.

During the tour, we visited four different cenotes. At one of them, we swam around an open cenote with goggles on to look at the fish and structure of the cenote beneath the surface of the water. At another, we ziplined across the cenote before eventually jumping in. At the third one, we kayaked around another long semi-open cenote. Finally, at the last one we rappelled down a pretty deep cenote and chilled in the cold water down there for a while. The whole experience of all this was really cool, and we had a good tour guide that talked about and explained the different types of cenotes that we visited.

Chichen Itza

Visiting these ruins was one of the coolest parts of our trip. Although it was really hot and humid, the beauty of the structures was enough for me to love it. I had never seen any sort of ruins before, so getting to see Chichen Itza was a new and surreal experience, and it made me understand why it's one of the seven wonders of the world. Learning about the architecture and some of it's design choices was really fascinating, especially the fact that the pyramid produces an echo that sounds like a quetzal at the clap of the hand. Another really cool part was seeing the structure for the Mayan Ball Game. While the rules of the game are not entirely known, it was still really interesting to see and learn about.
