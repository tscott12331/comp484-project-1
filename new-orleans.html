New Orleans

About New Orleans

Founded in 1718 under French colonial rule, New Orleans has a rich history and culture. 37 years after being obtained by the U.S in the Louisiana Purchase, New Orleans was the third most populous city in the country. Although the city no longer holds that position, it still holds over 380,000 people. From it's jazzy tunes to the delicious Creole food, the city has a lot to offer.

My Experience

I went to New Orleans in July 2022 as a summer family trip. Going into the trip, I really didn't know much about the city and was just tagging along with the family. During the trip, however, I found myself pleasantly surprised with all the things you can do in the city. Three of the favorite things that I did while on the trip were:

  • Airboats
  • Bourbon Street
  • Ghost Tour

My Favorite Parts of the Trip

Airboats

When I landed in New orleans, one of the last things that I thought I'd be doing was zooming on an airboat. So when my family decided that they wanted to go a little out of the way to do this, I really didn't know what to expect. When we got to the spot though, I knew it was going to be a good time. Not only did we drift around a swamp in an airboat going really fast, we also got to see a bunch of gators just swimming around. This part of the trip was probably the most unexpected, but also the most thrilling and adrenaline pumping.

French Quarter

French Quarter is a classic tourist location when visiting New Orleans, so we had to visit it. One of the main spots we went to was Bourbon Street. This was one of the most active places that I've ever been to. It was filled with people front to back with jazz playing all around. We also walked around quite a bit just to site see in the quarter. We saw the St. Louis Cathedral, explored the French Market, and got coffee and Cafe Du Monde.

Ghost Tour

Another excursion that I wasn't expecting to do on this trip was a ghost tour. I had no idea about the paranormal history and legends within the city, so it was pretty interesting to learn about it. While on the tour, we walked around different parts of the city while the tour guide talked about various legends and ghost stories. He showed us a bunch of supposed haunted spots, and the history behind them. Even though I'm not really a believer in ghosts, I still think it was a fun and interesting experience to have, and I definitely learned a good amount of history while on this tour.
