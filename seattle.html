Seattle

About Seattle

Seattle is a city in Washington founded in 1851. It is the 18th most populous city in the U.S, and the most populous in Washington with a population over 780,000. It's known for its prominence in performing arts as well as being the home of grunge music. Some major attractions in the city include the Space Needle, Pike Place Market, the boat rides, and much more.

My Experience

I visited Seattle back in 2022 for my 18th birthday. At the time, it had been a while since I had gone out of state for anything, so I was really looking forward to visiting Seattle to gain new experiences. One of the main things that drew me towards Seattle out of all places was the Space Needle, and the view that you get while up there. While visiting, however, I discovered that there was much more to the city that I wasn't even aware about, and found my self enjoying the trip even more than I thought I would.

Favorite Parts of the Trip

Space Needle

As I said before, this is what compelled me to go to Seattle. I really wanted to see the skyline from the top of this thing, and I definitely wasn't disappointed when I got there. I went up nearing sunset, so I got to see the view of the city in the daylight and at night. One thing that shocked me about the Space Needle was the rotating glass floor. I had no idea that this was a thing before getting there, but it was pretty cool being able to look straight down from the top. Since the floor was rotating, you could also just stand in one spot and get a constantly changing view.

One thing that I haven't mentioned yet is that I am somewhat afraid of heights. I was obviously aware of this before going up the Space Needle, and was willing to push through it to see the view. At first it was pretty scary, especially on the elevator ride up and when I first saw the glass floor. After being up there for a bit and walking around on the glass floor, I got used to it. What I didn't get used to, however, was the outside area. The constant wind and cold air while outside only reminded me of how high up I was. Along with that the slanted glass panes allowed you to lean a bit over the edge to see the shear drop. Even though I was terrified, I still managed to lean on the slanted glass for a few seconds.

Pike Place Market

After conquering my fear of heights the day prior, visiting the Pike Place Market was a big and much needed change of speed in my visit to Seattle. Seeing all the underground shops and what they had to offer was really cool, and it felt like it was never ending. After exploring all the shops, we went to see the infamous gum wall. Although pretty disgusting, I think it's pretty cool to get a visual of just how many people have been there through pieces of gum on a wall. Right after seeing the gum wall, we decided to get a nice cup of coffee at the Ghost Alley Espresso coffee shop right next to the wall. Overall, walking through the market was a fun and chill experience that I would reccomend to anybody.

Boat Ride

On our last day, we decided it would be nice to take a chill ride on a tour boat around the bay. I feel like this was the perfect thing to do as the trip came to a close because I got to see the city in its entirety from the outside with a nice tour guide talking about everything that we got to see. It was an hour long excursion with nice music, food, drinks, and a good tour guide.
